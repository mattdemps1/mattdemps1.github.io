Molecular Bit

A platform for engineered protein conformational switching — enabling a new category of therapeutics switchable from outside the body via external field actuation.

The Thesis

Most biologically active proteins work like a light with no switch — once present, they're on. There's no remote control for biology.

Molecular Bit is a platform for designing proteins engineered to exist in two stable conformational states and to toggle between them in response to an external field. This allows for temporal and spatial control of protein activity without additional drugs or genetic modification.

The development cycle is computational-first: generative AI proposes candidates, which are then screened through large-scale molecular dynamics (MD) validation. Our automated design pipeline evaluates hundreds of candidates per day, feeding high-confidence designs into active wet lab planning.

→ Status: Second-generation candidates, active wet lab planning.

UAV Predictive Object Tracking

First-authored conference paper. A system that gives drones "object permanence" — predicting where a target is even when they can't see it.

Aerospace Systems Lab · Indiana University

Drones lose situational awareness the moment a target disappears behind an obstacle or exits the camera frame. The system I built treats this like a human tracking problem — predicting where the target went, not just where it was.

The framework fuses camera data with inertial motion sensors to maintain a continuous target state estimate through visual gaps. When the target reappears, the drone is already pointed in the right direction.

Multi-State Protein Design

Designed a cap domain that physically closes over an enzyme's active site when a specific ligand is present — and opens again when it leaves. This gives the enzyme conditional selectivity: it only acts when the cap allows access.

Built automated screening tools to quantify ligand exposure across apo (open) and holo (closed) states, combining structural metrics with predicted signals to produce ranked candidate lists.
