Featured Project

Molecular Bit

Wireless, reversible modulation of protein activity using magnetoelectric nanoparticles.

Molecular Bit is a platform for building proteins that toggle between active and inactive states in response to an externally applied magnetic field — no drugs, no light, no genetic modification required. The system uses magnetoelectric nanoparticles (MENPs) as transducers: a magnetic field drives piezoelectric conversion into a local electric field that disrupts the electrostatic binding holding a designed protein domain against the nanoparticle surface, releasing it into its active conformation. The entire switch is wireless, reversible, and operates under physiological conditions.

The core infrastructure is in place. We have built an automated protein design pipeline (RFdiffusion → ProteinMPNN → ESMFold → Rosetta screening) and a molecular dynamics tool that evaluates candidates across three phases: equilibration in the OFF state, field-driven switching dynamics, and return verification. We are currently running our second-generation candidate pool, evaluating hundreds of designs per day.

Phase 01
Computational Design
Generative AI pipeline + MD screening. Ranked candidates with free-energy profiles.
Phase 02
Expression & Validation
20 candidates synthesized. Proxy surface screening to de-risk full MENP integration.
Phase 03
MENP Integration
Real magnetic field testing. 50+ switching cycles per lead. Performance characterization.
Phase 04
Functional Gating
Field-driven control of protease cleavage. Proof of generalizable biological gating.
